Journaling often feels effortful, repetitive, or unrewarding, and existing tools lack engaging structures that encourage reflection, growth, and follow-through.

Motivation

Studies have shown that regularly reflecting on both positive experiences and challenges can improve emotional well-being. Rose Garden makes journaling easier and more engaging by using the rose‚Äìbud‚Äìthorn reflection framework, where roses represent positive moments, buds represent things to look forward to, and thorns represent challenges or difficulties. Through these prompts, users plant their reflections in a digital garden, rewarding consistency and encouraging long-term reflection through visible growth and progress.

User Research & Insights

12 User Interviews

Through interviews with 8 digital artists and designers, we discovered that 75% of participants use 3+ different tools during their ideation phase, leading to fragmented workflows and lost ideas.

35 Survey Responses

Artists want a spatial, visual way to organize inspiration that mimics how they think. Traditional bookmarking tools feel too linear and disconnected from the creative process.

Key Features

Infinite Canvas

Built on Excalidraw's canvas, providing unlimited space for organizing visual references, sketches, and notes in a freeform, spatial layout that matches creative thinking.

Quick Capture

Drag and drop images, paste URLs, or quickly add text notes without leaving your canvas. Reduces friction in the inspiration-gathering process.

Visual Clustering

Group related ideas and references together visually. Create mood boards, organize by theme, or map out conceptual relationships spatially.

Seamless Integration

Export your canvas or portions of it to other tools. Works alongside your existing workflow rather than replacing it.

Design Process

The design evolved through multiple iterations based on user feedback. Early versions focused heavily on automation, but we discovered users wanted more control over their organizational system.

Technical Implementation

Built with React and TypeScript, leveraging Excalidraw's open-source canvas library as the foundation. Key technical challenges included optimizing performance with large numbers of images and implementing real-time collaboration features.

Architecture Highlights

Component-based architecture with custom hooks for canvas state management. Implemented lazy loading for images to maintain performance. Used Context API for global state management of user preferences and canvas data.

Challenges & Learnings

Technical Challenges

Managing canvas performance with hundreds of images required implementing virtualization and progressive loading strategies. Balancing feature richness with simplicity was an ongoing challenge throughout development.

Key Learnings

The importance of building with real user workflows in mind rather than assumed needs. Early and frequent user testing prevented us from over-engineering features that users didn't actually want.

Next Steps

Future iterations will focus on collaborative features, allowing teams to ideate together in shared canvas spaces. We're also exploring AI-powered suggestion features to help users discover related inspiration based on their existing canvas content.

Plans to integrate with popular design tools like Figma and Adobe Creative Cloud to create a more seamless workflow for professional designers.
